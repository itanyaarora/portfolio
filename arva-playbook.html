01The fundamentals

Founders: Dipalie Bajaj (Co-founder & CEO) and Nidhi Panchmal (Co-founder). Described in press as childhood friends who started exploring fertility together after family pressure about biological clocks. They visited IVF clinics with no medical background and found the experience disorienting. Inc42

Founded: 2022 (pilot). Fertility test launched January 2024, consultations March 2024.

HQ: Bengaluru. Flagship clinic in Whitefield. Plan: 10 clinics across India by 2027 (Mumbai + Delhi next).

Team size: ~10-15 people + a medical advisory panel.

Pricing:

  • Complete fertility test (11 hormones): ₹2,200-5,650
  • Fertility multivitamin: ₹1,799-4,799
  • Expert consultation: ₹500-1,000
  • Arva360 coaching: ₹10,000/month (food, supplements, workouts, weekly video coaching, daily WhatsApp)

Traction (Feb-Mar 2025): 4,000+ women served · 40,000-member community · 60% MoM growth · ~22K Instagram followers · $1M pre-seed in Feb/Mar 2025, led by All In Capital.

02The brand voice — how they talk

The dominant move is second-person address, never the third-person clinical patient. Their AMH explainer opens:

"You've likely come across the term 'AMH' — perhaps during a fertility consultation, a routine blood test, or while exploring options like egg freezing or IVF. But what is AMH, really? And how much does it actually tell you about your chances of getting pregnant?" — Arva blog: What is AMH?

The same explainer handles the most emotionally loaded moment — a low AMH result — with:

"First of all, don't panic. A low AMH result doesn't mean your fertility journey is over. You only need one good egg to conceive. AMH is just a data point." — Same blog

That's the move: acknowledge the feeling, reframe the data, restore agency. Not "the patient's ovarian reserve is below the reference range." Just "don't panic — you only need one good egg."

Vs. competitors

  • Veera Health leads with disease-state language ("treating PCOS for 110M women") — clinical authority. Arva leads with timeline + choice.
  • Luma Fertility uses similar warm/modern positioning but anchors in IVF clinics first. Arva starts pre-IVF, at home, hormones first.
  • Allara (US) is condition-and-insurance-led ("PCOS, virtual care, covered"). Arva is timeline-led.

The difference isn't the category — it's the starting verb. Veera "treats," Luma "performs IVF," Arva "helps you plan."

03Visual identity

  • Palette: the brand's own logo file is literally named ARVA_Merlot.png. A warm wine/burgundy as primary, not the femtech default millennial pink. Merlot reads as adult, considered, not girlboss — signals "this is a real decision, not a wellness fling."
  • Photography: weighted toward real bodies and warm interior clinic spaces. Arva's own copy uses "home, not a hospital" and "warm spaces." Avoids the stock-photo lab coat.
  • Type: clean modern sans, prioritising legibility over personality.
  • LinkedIn tagline: "Fertility care, reimagined for the next generation of India."
  • Product headline: "Know your fertility. Plan with confidence." — verb + noun + emotional outcome.
  • The results screen (lived experience): a 1:1 free fertility-coach session is bundled into every test. Results aren't a PDF you cry over alone — they're a conversation. That's structural, not cosmetic.

05The specific moves that built recall

"Two friends figuring it out together"

Dipalie + Nidhi being childhood friends with no medical background is the entire brand origin. Press picks it up because it answers "why should I trust you?" with "because we were you."

Founder-led content as a deliberate choice

Per Inc42: "The cofounders decided on a founder-led approach, with all their content being delivered directly by either Bajaj or Panchmal." This sparked viral videos with millions of views in the first month.

Arva Over Ice events

Real-world meetups (Bombay edition documented) where women came to "chat about egg freezing" but actually surfaced "expectations, aspirations, fears, and things I haven't been able to voice elsewhere." That last phrase is the brand promise as a customer testimonial.

Myth-busting as a content engine

"Egg freezing in India: myths, facts & what doctors won't always tell you" frames Arva against the dominant gatekeeper (the IVF clinic), not against rival startups.

"50% of fertility is emotional"

A repeatable line that licenses the brand to do non-clinical things (coaching, community, WhatsApp support) without seeming unserious.

06The non-judgmental moves, specifically

The core trick: separate the data from the verdict. AMH is "just a data point." A ₹5,650 test is information, not a diagnosis. The judgment is something you can opt out of.

07Founder learnings

  • They started with a pilot, not a product. "There was no initial plan to build Arva. We began Arva's journey with a pilot in 2022 to validate the actual problems women were facing." — Dipalie. Two years between formation and shipping the test.
  • They built the audience before the product. Social media presence first, fertility test second. This is the move Tanvi will recognise.
  • Hiring for brand: no public statement. Dipalie's background is UX design + HCI at SCAD + Harvard entrepreneurship cert, which probably explains why the brand looks like a product, not a clinic. Worth asking Dipalie directly.
  • What they got wrong: no public "we got this wrong" moment found in research. Worth a real ask if Tanya is in a 1:1 with Dipalie.

08Direct applications for Elfina

Mental-health-therapist-matching has the same brand problem fertility had: high stakes, high stigma, low information, an existing market (clinics, psychiatry) that talks down to the user. Seven moves Tanvi could try, framed as moves not templates.

  1. Pick a starting verb that isn't "treat." Arva says "plan." Veera says "treat PCOS." Elfina could say "match," "begin," "find your person," or "try therapy." The verb sets the entire tone — clinical or chosen.
  2. Second-person everything. Audit every onboarding screen, every email, every blog for "the patient" / "users with depression" / "individuals seeking care" and rewrite as "you." Free and immediate.
  3. Name the emotional moment before the data. Arva's "First of all, don't panic" before they explain low AMH. Elfina's equivalent: before a PHQ-9 score, before a "moderate depression" tag, before a therapist mismatch — a single human line. Mental health is more loaded than fertility, not less.
  4. Found a real-world ritual. Arva Over Ice = small, in-person, low-stakes, women-in-a-room. Elfina's version could be "Therapy Test Drive" or "First-Session Stories" evenings — not for selling, for surfacing the unsaid. The marketing return is the quotes you collect, which then run the next quarter of content.
  5. Make the founders the face. Dipalie and Nidhi front every video. Tanvi has a credible founder story — build it explicitly: "two friends, one struggle to find a therapist who got it" is the Arva template applied. Founder-led content is cheaper, more trusted, and more legally defensible in mental health than influencer content.
  6. Pick a palette that doesn't say "wellness app." Arva's merlot rejects pink. Elfina should reject calm-blue and sage-green — both are now generic. A single confident colour signals "we made a decision."
  7. Bundle a human into every transaction. Arva ships every test with a free 1:1 coach call. Elfina's equivalent: every therapist match comes with a no-cost 15-minute "is this the right fit?" call before the first paid session. Signal: we don't profit from a mismatch.
