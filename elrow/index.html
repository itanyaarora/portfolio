01

Sell the European Summer.

Europe's most colorful festival is coming to Bangalore.

European summer is the dominant aesthetic on Reels right now — Mediterranean palettes, Ibiza beach clubs, golden-hour parties. Elrow is literally Europe's biggest experiential festival, born in Barcelona. We don't manufacture the trend; we plug into it.

The execution: high-quality, premium-cut hero reels. Cinematic colour grading, beat-synced edits, treated like fashion campaign films. Footage stack pulled from Elrow's Ibiza, Madrid & London archives, layered over Bangalore plates for the "it's coming home" reveal. The reference photo above is the world — every frame should feel like that.

Why it cracks: The European summer cluster has algorithmic tailwind. Premium edits in a category already getting served = guaranteed reach.
02

The Color Caravan Campaign.

"Coming to Bangalore" — made physical.

An open-top vehicle rolls through Bangalore: a live DJ, three or four costumed Elrow characters, confetti cannons firing as it moves. Loud music, full theatre. Elrow's brand promise — colorful chaos — taken out of the venue and dropped onto the street.

The honest read on feasibility: a fully mobile activation is high-budget and high-permission. Bangalore Traffic Police, multi-jurisdiction police clearance, sound permits, RTO approval, music licensing — realistically 5–8 separate approvals over 3–4 weeks. So we plan for three versions, descending in budget and friction.

Tier A · The Full Caravan High budget · Hard permissions

Driving through Bangalore.

Full mobile activation across 5–6 high-foot-traffic stops — Indiranagar, Koramangala, Church Street, MG Road, UB City. Drone overhead, GoPro on truck, ground reaction cam. One shoot day yields 6–8 reels + an aftermovie + organic UGC + likely press pickup.

Risk: permits may not clear in time, or budget may not cover end-to-end production.

Tier B · Stationary Setup Mid budget · Easy permissions

Park-and-perform.

Same caravan, same characters, same DJ — but parked at a single high-impact location (UB City, 1MG, a cafe parking, an open-air venue). People come to the spectacle. Permits drop to property-owner approval + sound permit only. ~80% of the visual content, ~30% of the production friction.

Risk: lower organic spread without the moving-through-city angle.

Tier C · The VFX Version Lowest budget · Zero permissions

AI / VFX-led "what if."

If permits and budget both close down, we go fully VFX. AI-generated or composited shots of the caravan, characters, and confetti rolling down recognisable Bangalore streets — no real-world shoot needed.

Reference: the CRED "Bad Mobiles coming out of the garage" campaign that went viral two years ago — same playbook. A single, well-executed VFX shot can outperform a 6-figure production day if the concept is sharp.

Why it still works: the spectacle is the share trigger. Audiences don't care if it's real — they care if it's striking.

Why this tiered approach: we lock the concept now and de-risk execution. Whichever tier clears budget and permissions, the campaign still ships on time.
