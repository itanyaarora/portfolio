04 · Why fertility

Three partners on the table. Fertility goes first.

Why fertility first?
Age fit. 25 to 40 years old. Squarely in Elfina's sweet spot.
Client mindset. ~78% report emotional distress. IVF is an emotional chapter, and seeking therapy here feels natural, even respectable. Patients are already open to help.
Referral hook. Most chains have no in-house counselling. Therapy is a natural add-on, not competition.
Offer therapy inside that moment, and uptake clears 90%.
05 · Why not gynaecologists

A real channel, but not the first bet.

Elfina Health
Fertility clinics
Gynaecologists
Psychiatrists
Why not gynaecologists?
Age fit. Teenagers to postmenopausal women. Far broader than Elfina's 25-to-40 band.
Client mindset. Wide-ranging concerns, and some do link to mental health (postpartum depression, PCOS anxiety), but these aren't widely discussed yet. The conversation is only just opening up.
Referral hook. Therapy doesn't come up in a 15-minute consult. The referral feels forced.
Not a bad channel. Just not the first one to build.
06 · Why not psychiatrists

Already served, and a different patient.

Elfina Health
Fertility clinics
Gynaecologists
Psychiatrists
Why not psychiatrists?
Age fit. All ages, skews older. Broader than Elfina's 25-to-40 target.
Client mindset. Already in mental health care, but it's primarily medical and medication-led. Therapy runs in parallel. The need exists, but the patient is already being served.
Referral hook. Competition, not complement. Psychiatrists may see a therapy platform as encroaching on their practice.
The right channel to revisit later, not to lead with.

13 · Risks & constraints

What I'd watch.

Competition

Half the major IVF chains already advertise in-house counselling, so the play is the half that don't. Elfina's wedge: matched clinical therapy, a tier deeper.

Time-bounded value

A fertility patient's therapy need fades when the journey ends, a shorter lifetime value than postpartum into parenting.

Legality

Indian medical-conduct rules prohibit paying for referrals. The model is a partnership, not a paid channel; money flows clinic-to-Elfina for a service delivered.

The economics

Everything rests on Elfina's revenue per patient and the matching-call conversion rate, the number to confirm before scaling.
